Compliance
The Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) is a set of requirements for companies that process, store or transmit credit card information. PCI DSS is governed by PCI Security Standards Council consisting of independent members created by Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover and JCB.

The PCI DSS Compliance Service provides a necessary framework for developing a complete payment card data security process that includes prevention, detection, and appropriate reaction to security incidents.

KEY ANALYZE

01.

PCI compliance means you are contributing to a global payment card data security solution.

02.

PCI Compliance ensures that your systems are secure and free from security breaches and payment card thefts.

03.

PCI Compliance instills customer trust leading to customer confidence and repeat customers.

04.

PCI Compliance aids in preventing security breaches and payment card data theft in the present and in the future.

05.

In the process of meeting PCI Compliance, you’re prepared to comply with additional regulations.

06.

PCI Compliance helps contribute to corporate security strategies and improving IT infrastructure efficiency.

WHAT WE CAN DO FOR YOU?

CSL can help you meet the stringent requirements of PCI DSS setout by the PCI Security Standard Council. Our panel of both technical and process experts can perform the Gap Assessment in validating your organization’s compliance to PCI DSS standards and can set out a guiding stone to achieve PCI DSS . The daunting compliance process of PCI DSS Compliance Service is made simple without missing out on any of the PCI DSS standards requirements.

PCI DSS COMPLIANCE PROCESS

Identify the levels of PCI DSS Compliance
Based on the annual number of credit or debit card transactions in a business process. The classification level determines what an organization should be compliant to.

PCI DSS COMPLIANCE LEVELS

LEVEL 01.

6M Transactions / Year

LEVEL 02.

1 - 6M Transactions / Year

LEVEL 03.

20K - 1M Transactions / Year

LEVEL 04.

< 20K Transactions / Year
